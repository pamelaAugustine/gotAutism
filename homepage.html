ABOUT US

Raising Awareness

Autism is a spectrum disorder, not a Disease."Spectrum" in Autism spectrum disorder refers to the wide range of symptoms, skills, and severity of the disorder. The three common disorders on the Autism spectrum are Autism, Asperger's syndrome, and pervasive development disorder. The disability may range from mild to severe. Children affected by Autism have an excess of synapses, or connections between brain cells. This is because there is a shutdown in the natural pruning process that occurs during brain development. A typical pruning process involves eliminating about half of cortical synapses by late adolescence. Cortical synapses occur in the cortex, which is central to thought and processing information from the senses.

Early warning signs and symptoms of Autism are recognizable. If parents or doctors are able to diagnose Autism as an infant, treatment can greatly improve a baby's brain. Signs of Autism usually appear between 12 and 18 months, but parents should still be on the lookout for common Autism symptoms.

Diagnosis

If you are considering getting your child evaluated for Autism do some research. Doctors usually have a limited awareness to the disorder other than a definition. This is becoming more uncommon with the growing number of children diagnosed each year, but its important to find a doctor with experience treating patients with Autism as there are many special needs. A general practitioner is unable to diagnosis but is able to give the referral most insurance requires to see the specialists needed for evaluation. This usually involves an appointment with a neurologist to arrange an overnight EKG, and overall evaluation, combined with a practitioner for observation and questionnaire.


RESOURCES

Support groups are a great resource for parents who are new to the Autism community. They provide helpful tips on daily struggles, questions about what to expect, information on local establishments that are "Autism friendly", play dates, and overall parent support. Overall support groups help you understand that you are not alone and that there is hope in the individual living a fulfilling life.


THERAPY

Most common therapies involved in helping individuals with Autism include speech and occupational. Recent therapies have become available in certain areas involving music, sensory, pet, and horses. These therapies help the individual to learn to have the basic skills needed to become as integrated as possible. An associated disorder commonly found with autism is "sensory integration dysfunction". This causes the repetitive motions/actions such as spinning, jumping, and flapping. This is a condition that takes specialized therapy to treat as all sensory input is very different to individual. Therapies can include weighted vests, swings, "chew toys", headphones, and many others to help them feel grounded.
